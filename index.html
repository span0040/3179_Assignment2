World Happiness Report 2021


The World Happiness Report is an annual report that contains rankings and analysis of national happiness, based on respondent ratings of their own lives.

The main source of data is the Gallup World Poll, which contains life evaluation categories such as the Life Ladder Score, Logarithmized GDP per capita, Social Support, Healthy Life Expectancy, Freedom to make life choices, and Corruption perception from respondents from every country polled.

Life Ladder Score

The Life Ladder Score is derived from asking respondents to evaluate their current life as a whole using the image of a ladder, with the best possible life for them as a 10 and the worst possible as a 0.

From the rankings, we can see that Western Europe, North America and ANZ dominate the top end, whereas on the bottom end Sub-Saharan Africa is the most common region at the bottom of the rankings.

As for the change in scores for the past year, surprisingly almost all scores across the board are on an upward trend. This shows that countries have been resilient in facing the COVID-19 pandemic.

Logarithmized GDP per capita

The statistics of GDP per capita (variable name gdp) in purchasing power parity (PPP) at constant 2017 international dollar prices are from the October 14, 2020 update of the World Development Indicators (WDI).

The GDP rankings are led by a large percentage of countries coming from Western Europe, with a sole representative of the Middle Eastern and North Africa region in the United Arab Emirates being in the top 3.

Due to COVID-19, it is not a surprise that the GDP of countries across the board have dipped for 2020. With the world slowly recovering, it will be interesting to see which countries are able to bounce back.

Social Support

Social Support is the metric that is derived from the respondents'answer to the question "If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them or not?"

Being a binary question (either yes or no) the score is tallied from the average of the responses.

Social support scores at the top ranks mostly come from countries in Western Europe, as well as north America and ANZ. At the bottom, a large amount of countries seem to come from the Sub-Saharan African region.

For the change trends in this category, it seems to be an overall increase in social support scores for most countries, with some decreasing scores as well.

Healthy Life Expectancy

The Healthy Life Expectancy metric is the statistical measure of how long on average a person is expected to live based on current age and sex specific death rates.

For this report, the data was taken from the World Health Organisation's Global Health Observatory data repository.

Japan leads this category, but most countries at the top end still come from the western European region. Similarly, countries at the bottom of the rankings come from the Sub-Saharan African region.

Despite the global pandemic, life expectancies seem to be unaffected, with some countries moving in an upward change trend.

Freedom to make life choices

Freedom to make life choices is the national average of responses to the question: "Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?"

The rankings at the top end are slightly more diverse with multiple regions making an appearance. Same goes for the bottom end, with a big country such as Russia featuring in the bottom 10.

In terms of change trends, there is a lot more variation between countries and regions. This is as the score heavily depends on the governance and culture of countries.

Corruption perception

The Corruption perception metric is derived from the national average of these two questions: "“Is corruption widespread throughout the government or not" and “Is corruption widespread within businesses or not?”

For this category, the higher ranking determines the country that has the highest perceived corruption, and same for the bottom ranks. There is a lot less correlation between region and ranking, with scores varying between high and low from countries from the same region.

As a result, the change trends for this category do not have any correlation to their region or the COVID-19 pandemic, and is specific to the governance and culture of the country.
