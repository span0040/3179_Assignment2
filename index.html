World Happiness Report 2021


The World Happiness Report is an annual report that contains rankings and analysis of national happiness, based on respondent ratings of their own lives.

The main source of data is the Gallup World Poll, which contains life evaluation categories such as the Life Ladder Score, Logarithmized GDP per capita, Social Support, Healthy Life Expectancy, Freedom to make life choices, and Corruption perception from respondents from every country polled.

Life Ladder Score

The Life Ladder Score is derived from asking respondents to evaluate their current life as a whole using the image of a ladder, with the best possible life for them as a 10 and the worst possible as a 0.

Logarithmized GDP per capita

The statistics of GDP per capita (variable name gdp) in purchasing power parity (PPP) at constant 2017 international dollar prices are from the October 14, 2020 update of the World Development Indicators (WDI).

GDP per capita ranking

Social Support

Social Support is the metric that is derived from the respondents'answer to the question "If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them or not?"

Being a binary question (either yes or no) the score is tallied from the average of the responses.

Social Support score ranking

Consists of: Top 10 and Bottom 10 Ladder score ranked countries, as well as additional notable countries

Healthy Life Expectancy

The Healthy Life Expectancy metric is the statistical measure of how long on average a person is expected to live based on current age and sex specific death rates.

For this report, the data was taken from the World Health Organisation's Global Health Observatory data repository.

Healthy life expectancy ranking

Consists of: Top 10 and Bottom 10 Ladder score ranked countries, as well as additional notable countries

Freedom to make life choices

Freedom to make life choices is the national average of responses to the question: "Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?"

Corruption perception

The Corruption perception metric is derived from the national average of these two questions: "“Is corruption widespread throughout the government or not" and “Is corruption widespread within businesses or not?”
